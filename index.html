Alexis Langlois-Rémillard

Hausdorff postdoc at the Hausdorff Center for Mathematics, Universität Bonn

Since October 2023, I am a Hausdorff postdoc at the Hausdorff Center in Mathematics in Bonn under the mentorship of Eveliina Peltola.

From May 2023 to September 2023 I was a postdoc at Center for Scalable Data Analytics and Artificial Intelligence (ScaDS.AI) Leipzig, Universität Leipzig, and a visitor at the Max-Planck-Institut für Mathematik in den Naturwissenschaften, Leipzig, Germany, with Érika Roldán Roa.

From April 2019 to April 2023, I was a PhD student at Universiteit Gent under the supervision of Hendrik De Bie, Roy Oste and Joris Van der Jeugt. My thesis was on the representation theory of the Dunkl total angular momentum algebra.

From September 2014 to January 2019, I did my bachelor and master degrees at Université de Montréal, the latter under the direction of Yvan Saint-Aubin with a thesis on the representation theory of seam algebras, a generalisation of Temperley-Lieb algebras.

Research interests

I am interested in: algebra, representation theory, mathematical physics. More specifically, I study and work with: diagrammatic algebras and categories, centraliser algebras, rational Cherednik algebras, reflection groups, Dunkl operators, Lie (super)algebras, and Temperley-Lieb algebras. I have also recently been interested in complexity theory and extremal combinatorics through chess-inspired mathematical problems.
