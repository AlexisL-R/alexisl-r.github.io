Alexis Langlois-Rémillard

Ph.D. student at Universiteit Gent

Since April 2019, I am working at Universiteit Gent in the broad topic of representation theory of algebras with a focus on operator algebras and application to mathematical physics. My supervisors are Joris Van der Jeugt and Hendrik De Bie.

I did my bachelor and master degrees at Université de Montréal, the latter under the direction of Yvan Saint-Aubin on the representation theory of seam algebras, a generalization of Temperley-Lieb algebras.

Research interests

I am now working on the algebraic underlying structures of operator algebras. The mathematics areas involved are plenty but I concentrate on representation theory and Lie superalgebras. A long-term goal is to investigate the potential of diagrammatic algebras in this topic via the Hecke algebras framework and quantum algebras. More detailed introductory notes to come in my research notes.

    2019-09-10. We recently put our paper The representation theory of seam algebras on arXiv (link: 19.09.03499)! If you are interested in the representation theory of diagrammatic algebras, want an example of the use of cellular algebras or simply enjoy looking at diagrams, then it is the paper for you! The work synthesizes my master's thesis on seam algebras. We study the representation theory of a deformation of the classical Temperley-Lieb algebras that stems from left- and right-multiplication by Wenzl-Jones projectors.
