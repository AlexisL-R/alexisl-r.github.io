Welcome to the website of the Kleine seminar! This small seminar takes place at Universiteit Gent and was initiated in summer 2019 to create a space for graduate students and postdoc researchers with shared research interests to study recent subject in depth. The method is quite simple: each semester, the members meet and decide of one subject they want to investigate, then they choose a base reference and divide the subject amongst themselves so that each of them will present a part. It aims to keep an informal ambiance and every kind of questions is welcomed, especially dumb ones!

Current topic, Fall 2019

Hecke algebras and double affine Hecke algebras

This semester topic is on Hecke algebras with a view toward double affine Hecke algebras (DAHA). The reference we are following is the book Iwahori-Hecke Algebra and Schur Algebras of the Symmetric Group by Andrew Mathas and Pavel Etingof's course notes on DAHA. Some more references are in the shared files, only available for UGent members. We will complete in Mathas chapters 1 to 3 completely, part of chapter 4 and add some material on DAHA after.

We hope to run the seminar weekly for each speaker with possible breaks in between. The current location is Leslokaal 2.3 S8, the specific date and time is still under review.

The presentation will be on the section 3.4 on irreducible modules classification. This talk will conclude chapter 3 by proving the theorem of James and Dipper (3.43).
