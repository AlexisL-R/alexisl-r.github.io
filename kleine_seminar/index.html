Welcome to the website of the Kleine seminar! This small seminar takes place at Universiteit Gent and was initiated in summer 2019 to create a space for graduate students and postdoc researchers with shared research interests to study recent subject in depth. The method is quite simple: each semester, the members meet and decide of one subject they want to investigate, then they choose a base reference and divide the subject amongst themselves so that each of them will present a part. It aims to keep an informal ambiance and every kind of questions is welcomed, especially dumb ones!

Current topic, Fall 2019

Hecke algebras and double affine Hecke algebras

This semester topic is on Hecke algebras with a view toward double affine Hecke algebras (DAHA). The reference we are following is the book Iwahori-Hecke Algebra and Schur Algebras of the Symmetric Group by Andrew Mathas and Pavel Etingof's course notes on DAHA. Some more references are in the shared files, only available for UGent members.

We hope to run the seminar weekly for each speaker with possible break in between. The current location is Leslokaal 1.1 S9 Sterre campus, UGent, the specific date and time is still under review.

Next meeting Friday October 4th 14h00 on Chapter 2: Cellular algebras. The presenter is Alexis Langlois-Rémillard

The subject covered in this session will aim to be the first section of Chapter 2, stopping at Theorem 2.16. Alexis suggested that interested members should consult Appendix A of the book for a refreshment (or initiation) in representation theory of algebras. Also, it would be wise to read beforehand the definition of cellular algebras and work out example (i) of 2.2.
