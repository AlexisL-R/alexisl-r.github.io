Welcome to the website of the Kleine seminar! This small seminar takes place at Universiteit Gent and was initiated in summer 2019 to create a space for graduate students and postdoc researchers with shared research interests to study recent subject in depth. The method is quite simple: each semester, the members meet and decide of one subject they want to investigate, then they choose a base reference and divide the subject amongst themselves so that each of them will present a part. It aims to keep an informal ambiance and every kind of questions is welcomed, especially dumb ones!

Present topic, Fall 2020 (October - December, continuing Winter 2020)

Categorification, an exemplar view

The subject for this semester is on categorification. It is an active research subject that regroup many approaches loosely tied together by an idea: that you can get more information by considering an higher algebraic structure for a mathematical object. The interest spreads from the physical community in the early 1990.

We hope to study the subject using examples as guiding line to navigate the subtleties. The main examples used will be (double affine) Hecke algebras, quantum groups and Lie algebras. The prerequisites to the subject are:

  • Category theory basic vocabulary (Category, functors and natural transformation)
  • Bases in theory of Lie and associative algebras
  • What is an Hecke algebra (of type A)

  • Lauda, A., 2011. An introduction to diagrammatic algebra and categorified quantum sl(2) 7 (review of ideas contained in other of his article)

  • Savage, A., 2018. String diagrams and categorification. arXiv:1806.06873 [math]. (Lecture notes from a mini course)

  • Khovanov, M., Mazorchuk, V., Stroppel, C., 2009 A Brief Review of Abelian Categorifications 31. TAC. (A short overview of categorifications with plenty of examples, see: http://www.tac.mta.ca/tac/volumes/22/19/22-19.pdf
