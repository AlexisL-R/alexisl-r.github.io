Welcome to the website of the Kleine seminar! This small seminar takes place at Universiteit Gent and was initiated in summer 2019 to create a space for graduate students and postdoc researchers with shared research interests to study recent subject in depth. The method is quite simple: each semester, the members meet and decide of one subject they want to investigate, then they choose a base reference and divide the subject amongst themselves so that each of them will present a part. It aims to keep an informal ambiance and every kind of questions is welcomed, especially dumb ones!

Resources for the seminar

Etingof, Gelaki, Nikshych and Ostrik have written recently a book on tensor categories (see https://pages.uoregon.edu/vostrik/ and navigate to the book). In the preface, they outline a possible one-semester course that we could do to cover this exciting material. Here is it with minor touch
Chapter 1Homework and brief recap (1.1 to 1.9)
Chapter 22.1 to 2.10
Chapter 3Skip unless needed
Chapter 44.1 to 4.9
Chapter 55.1 to 5.6, more if wanted
Chapter 66.1 to 6.3
Chapter 77.1 to 7.12
Chapter 88.1 to 8.14
Chapter 99.1 to 9.9 with 9.12
Do not hesitate to use other sources such as Deligne's work, Mac Lane, Etingof and Ostrik, Joyal or Drinfeld etc.
